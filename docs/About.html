Meet

Clinton

Aspiring Ph.D. Mathematics Candidate

I am an aspiring Mathematics Ph.D. candidate with a deep commitment to making mathematics accessible to all and discovering its connections across cultures and disciplines. I graduated from the Federal University of Petroleum Resources, Effurun (FUPRE), with a B.Sc. in Mathematics, where I was honored to receive the Dean's Prize for best graduating student in the College of Science, recognition as the best graduating Mathematics undergraduate, and served as class valedictorian.

Building on this academic background, I had the privilege of serving as President of the Mathematical Association, a role that allowed me to foster a collaborative learning environment for students, faculty, and the broader mathematical community. I supported fellow students in their mathematical journeys through free tutorials and mentorship programs, further deepening my dedication to making mathematical knowledge inclusive. During my tenure as departmental president, I also created the FUPRE Math Escape Room and FUPRE Math Career Fair to enhance student engagement in mathematical problem-solving and help prepare them for transition into the labor market.

As part of my ever-growing commitment to creating an inclusive and welcoming mathematical space, I have remained dedicated to capacity building. This has involved creating a program that connects undergraduate researchers with experienced graduate students in their fields of interest, tutoring undergraduate mathematics courses for free, and organizing bootcamps on mathematical software including SageMath, GAP, Maxima, Maple, Matlab, and Mathematica, as well as programming languages such as R and Python, and technical writing with LaTeX. In addition to these initiatives, I have had the privilege of teaching graduate students to code in Python, LaTeX, and Matlab, and I take pride in having been recognized for excellence in mathematical analysis and abstract algebra courses.

Following graduation, I joined the Ugbene lab as a Research Assistant under Dr. Ifeanyichukwu Ugbene, Head of the Mathematics Department at the Federal University of Petroleum Resources. Concurrently, I completed my National Youth Service Corps year as a mathematics teacher at Grace College in Benin City, where I taught SS1-SS3 students preparing for their certificate examinations. Witnessing an 88% increase in mathematics examination performance among the 2024 cohort was deeply rewarding, reinforcing my conviction that clear instruction and genuine investment in students can transform outcomes.

Moreover, recognizing the severe shortage of math educators in rural schools, I spearheaded an initiative to mobilize fellow corps members and provide supplementary instruction to students in underserved communities. This effort allowed us to teach students facing a deficit of qualified teachers at least twice weekly, effectively giving back to the community that supported our growth. My obligation to make mathematics accessible to all extends beyond the four walls of the university, into the communities that shaped me. This has led me to create a teaching program that brings mathematical concepts to children in riverine communities across the Niger Delta, literally bringing mathematics education to every doorstep. Through this program, we teach modular arithmetic using the natural rhythms of tidal patterns in the sea and rivers during school holidays, connecting abstract mathematical ideas to the world these children know intimately. This work has also inspired a research initiative that explores the Ife divination system, an ancient Niger Delta practice based on zeros and ones that predates George Boole's formalization of Boolean algebra. By converting this system into modern Boolean algebra frameworks, I'm contributing to ethno-mathematics while discovering new patterns applicable to binary neural networks in machine learning.

In the past, during my bachelor's thesis research, I worked in the field of Operations Research, with specific focus on lateral transshipment in supply chains and facility location optimization problems under the supervision of Dr. Samuel Zelibe, which marked the beginning of my documented research journey. Prior to this, I had developed an interest in mathematical biology through an internship at FUPRE, where I investigated mild cognitive impairment using Boolean networks. Now, I employ tools from semigroup theory, graph theory, network science, stochastic processes, optimization, differential equations, and numerical methods to explore problems in biology and develop impactful solutions. I have also expanded my skill set through coursework on DataCamp in Data Analytics, Data Science, and Machine Learning. And, I am equally committed to applying my skills toward challenging problems in Mathematical Biology research and pursuing Data Science roles within the domain of AI for Science, where I can contribute meaningfully to interdisciplinary discovery.
