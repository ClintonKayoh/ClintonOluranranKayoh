Services & Community Outreach

Building Bridges Through Mathematics

Discover how I'm working to make mathematics accessible, engaging, and culturally relevant from classrooms to riverine communities, from ancient divination systems to modern neural networks. Every initiative is driven by a simple belief: mathematics belongs to everyone.

President of the Mathematical Association

I had the privilege of serving as President of the Mathematical Association, a role that provided a platform to significantly enhance the academic and social environment for the entire mathematical community. My primary achievement was fostering a collaborative learning environment that actively bridged the communication and engagement gap between students, faculty, and the broader community.

This involved organizing regular seminars, leading student-faculty mixers, and initiating peer-led study groups that increased engagement in challenging upper-division courses. The tenure was focused on institutionalizing support structures, ensuring that the association functioned not just as a club, but as a central hub for mathematical scholarship and professional networking.

Undergraduate Research Mentorship Program (DiscoverU)

My dedication to capacity building led to the creation of a program that connects undergraduate researchers with experienced graduate students in their fields of interest. Recognizing the steep learning curve and lack of formal guidance often faced by undergraduates entering research, I designed this initiative to provide targeted, specialized mentorship.

This program has been pivotal in creating a sustainable research pipeline, significantly increasing the number of undergraduates who successfully complete and present their first research projects.

Program Impact

Graduate students act as technical advisors and role models, assisting with everything from literature review and methodology design to code development and conference presentation preparation.

FUPRE Math Escape Room

To enhance student engagement in mathematical problem-solving, I created the FUPRE Math Escape Room. This project was conceived as an innovative, low-stakes environment where students could apply complex mathematical concepts ranging from linear algebra and differential equations to logic and cryptography in a fun, gamified setting.

The core objective was to dismantle the perception of mathematics as purely abstract and demonstrate its real-world application through puzzles and challenges. The Escape Room became an immensely popular annual event, boosting collaborative skills, critical thinking, and a deeper appreciation for the practical utility of mathematical theory.

FUPRE Math Career Fair

Recognizing the challenges undergraduate students face in translating their math degrees into career opportunities, I formed the FUPRE Math Career Fair. The goal was simple but essential: to prepare undergraduates for the transition into the labor market for math-related jobs.

I brought together alumni, industry professionals, and recruiters from diverse sectors (finance, technology, data science) to conduct workshops on technical interviewing, portfolio building, and salary negotiation. This initiative not only provided students with essential professional development but also highlighted the vast, often unseen, career paths available to mathematically trained graduates.

Graduate Student Technical Instruction

Extending technical education to the highest level, I have had the privilege of teaching graduate students to code in Python, LaTeX, and Matlab. This instruction was often tailored to meet the immediate needs of advanced research, helping students apply sophisticated coding principles to simulate models, process large datasets, and critically write their dissertations and papers using professional-grade technical documentation.

Bringing Math to Riverine Communities

This work led to my most innovative outreach initiative: creating a teaching program that literally brings mathematical concepts to children in riverine communities across the Niger Delta. Recognizing that traditional classroom models fail these communities during school holidays, we use a mobile, seasonal approach.

By connecting abstract mathematical ideas to the world these children know intimately; the rise and fall of the water, we foster a culturally relevant, unforgettable learning experience that demystifies mathematics and demonstrates its presence in their daily lives.

Innovative Pedagogy

The curriculum uniquely teaches modular arithmetic using the natural rhythms of tidal patterns in the sea and rivers.

Ethno-mathematics Research: Ife Divination System

This dedicated community work has inspired a critical research initiative that explores the Ife divination system, an ancient Niger Delta practice based on zeros and ones that fundamentally predates George Boole's formalization of Boolean algebra. Through rigorous mathematical conversion, I'm translating this complex, cultural system into modern Boolean algebra and finite field frameworks.

By analyzing the Ife system's underlying patterns, I am discovering new structures and efficiency principles with tangible applications in modern computational science. This project is a powerful synthesis of cultural preservation, abstract mathematics, and cutting-edge technology.

Dual Impact

First: Contributing to ethno-mathematics by preserving and demonstrating how African intellectual traditions engaged with concepts foundational to computer science centuries ago.

Second: Opening new avenues in coding theory and demonstrating relevance to binary neural networks in machine learning.
